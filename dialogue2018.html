    'Reinvention' of Literary Networks by Moretti

    “Networks are made of vertices and edges; plot networks, of characters and verbal exchanges. In plays this works well, because words are deeds, deeds are almost always words, and so, basically, a network of speech acts is a network of actions”.

    Franco Moretti. “Distant Reading”

    Drama Corpora in TEI

    (facilitating the automated extraction of network data)

    • Théâtre Classique: 1090 plays from the 17th and 18th century
    • Shakespeare His Contemporaries: 860 plays written between 1550 and 1700
    • German Drama Corpus: 466 German-language plays from 1730 till 1930
    • Letteratura teatrale nella Biblioteca italiana: 171 Italian plays
    • Russian Drama Corpus: 90 Russian-language plays from 1740s till 1940s
    • Dramawebben: 62 Swedish plays

    Markup => Network


    • network data = interactions between characters
    • "Two characters interact with one another if they perform a speech act within the same segment of a drama (usually a 'scene')." (following an idea of Solomon Marcus, "Poetica matematică", 1970)
